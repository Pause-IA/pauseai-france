The number of people aware of AI risks is still limited.
You are now part of that group.

**Your actions carry more weight than you imagine.** Whether you have five
minutes or several hours a week, there is a useful way to contribute. Here is
where to start.

## Start here

The actions that matter most, in order of impact:

1. **<a href="/{lang}/rejoindre">Join Pause AI</a>**: this is the most important
   step. You coordinate your actions with the rest of the movement and multiply
   your impact.
2. **<a href="/{lang}/ecrire-a-mes-elus">Write to your representatives</a>**: a
   ready-to-personalise email for your MP and senator. Letters from citizens are
   surprisingly effective.
3. **<a href="/{lang}/groupes-locaux">Join a local group</a>**: take action near
   you (leafleting, protests, outreach) or start a group in your city.
4. **<a href="/{lang}/dons">Make a donation</a>**: our campaigns, events and
   day-to-day operations rely on the support of our donors. Our independence
   from the industry is total.

## What if...

### You are comfortable speaking publicly

- **Try to convince a member of government to work on an international moratorium.** It is the most important thing you can do.
- **Try to convince journalists** to write about AI safety.
- **Reach out to influencers** to get them to cover the topic and make videos about AI safety.
- **Ask the leadership** of any official body you belong to to take a stance on this issue at an institutional level.

### You are an elected official or work for the government

- **Learn about the [problem](/{lang}/dangers) and its [solutions](/{lang}/propositions).**
- **Form coalitions with other countries.**
- **Invite the leaders of AI labs** to parliamentary hearings so they explain how they anticipate the potential catastrophic risks of AI, and on what timeline.
- **Set up a committee** to study the [risks of AI](/{lang}/dangers).

### You know the law (or international law)

- **Help draft legislation**: [propose model texts](https://www.campaignforaisafety.org/celebrating-the-winners-law-student-moratorium-treaty-competition/). ([examples](https://futureoflife.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/FLI_Policymaking_In_The_Pause.pdf), [proposals](https://www.openphilanthropy.org/research/12-tentative-ideas-for-us-ai-policy/))
- **Take part in the debate** when the government seeks citizens' input on AI policy.

### You can build for the web

- **[Improve this site](https://github.com/Pause-IA/pauseai-france)**: the code is open and contributions are welcome.

### You work in the AI sector

- **Do not work on superintelligence**: if you have a breakthrough idea to make AI systems even faster, please do not develop it, share it, or talk about it. We need to slow down the development of the most dangerous models, not speed it up.
- **Talk about AI risks to your management and colleagues**: push them to take a stance at an institutional level.
- **Organise a seminar** on AI safety at your workplace. For inspiration, [watch these videos](https://pauseai.info/learn#videos).
- **Sign** the [Statement on AI Risk](https://www.safe.ai/work/statement-on-ai-risk).

### You work in AI safety

If you are new to AI alignment, unless you are extremely qualified or have a flash of genius in the field, **seriously consider campaigning for a pause instead**. Without a moratorium, you simply will not have the time to grow your alignment career to the point where you can truly be useful.

If you are already a recognised figure in alignment, why not [make your voice heard more in the public square](https://twitter.com/TrustlessState/status/1651538022360285187) and lend your name to calls for a moratorium and regulation of the sector?

## Tips for being effective

- **Be brave and direct** in your public statements: avoid watered-down language. Your first instinct should not be to hedge. You can add caveats if asked, but do not give the impression that you are not worried if you are.
- **Be less demanding of yourself**: apply [the Pareto principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pareto_principle). Avoid the classic mistake of polishing a perfect document for months. Launch your project publicly and adapt as you go. There is no time to waste.

Keep this in mind: any other action would be the equivalent of rearranging deck chairs on the Titanic. We need to run for the bridge, grab the wheel and steer away from the iceberg. We may not have much time, but we can try. We can do this!
